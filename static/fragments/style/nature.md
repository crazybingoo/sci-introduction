# Style Fragment: Nature

Use this fragment for Nature-style or high-impact SCI Introduction writing.

- Start from the phenomenon, task, system, disease burden, or scientific problem.
- Avoid "X is important" boilerplate.
- Move quickly from field stake to unresolved problem.
- Explain why the gap persists, not merely that few studies exist.
- Synthesize prior work by capability and limitation, not author-by-author.
- Make the "Here we" or "In this study" sentence answer the gap directly.
- Keep method details only where needed to make the present move intelligible.
- Use the final paragraph for grouped evidence preview and bounded contribution.
- Avoid broad claims of causality, mechanism, clinical utility, general superiority, or universal applicability unless the body supports them.
