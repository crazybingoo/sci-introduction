# Core Workflow

1. Classify the request as `diagnose`, `draft`, `rewrite`, or `coach`.
2. Detect language and style axes from the manifest.
3. Load only the selected static fragments.
4. Check body-first readiness.
5. Ask or reconstruct the three video-guided questions:
   - Why is this field, problem, system, or task important?
   - What specific problem remains unresolved?
   - What does this study investigate, test, build, or explain?
6. Build a one-line argument:

   `field stake -> bottleneck -> unresolved gap -> present study -> evidence preview -> bounded contribution`

7. Identify mismatches between the intended story and what the body supports.
8. Choose a 4-6 paragraph funnel, usually 5 paragraphs.
9. Draft, rewrite, diagnose, or coach according to the selected task fragment.

## Default Paragraph Jobs

1. Field stake.
2. Bottleneck.
3. Prior attempts and residual gap.
4. Present move.
5. Evidence preview and bounded claim.

## Red Lines

- Do not draft from the Abstract alone.
- Do not inflate significance beyond the body.
- Do not list papers one by one when synthesis is possible.
- Do not turn the final paragraph into a figure roadmap.
- Do not invent citations, sample sizes, statistics, limitations, or results.
